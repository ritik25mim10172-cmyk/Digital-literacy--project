Title: Fake Payment Screenshot Scam

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick people into believing they have received money. One typical method involves fake payment screenshots.

In this scam, the fraudster first contacts the victim, often pretending to be a buyer on platforms like OLX or Instagram. They agree to purchase an item and ask for the seller’s UPI ID. Instead of actually transferring money, they send a fake payment screenshot showing a successful transaction. Sometimes, they also create urgency by saying “check quickly” or “network delay hai.”

Step-by-step, the scam works like this:

Fraudster finds a seller online.
Initiates conversation and gains trust.
Sends a fake payment screenshot.
Pressures the victim to deliver the product quickly.
Victim realizes later that no money was received.

College students and small online sellers are common targets because they often rely on quick digital payments and may not verify transactions properly.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal details shared during the transaction. In some cases, repeated scams can lead to larger fraud networks exploiting victims.

This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of verifying payments directly in your bank or UPI app instead of trusting screenshots.
